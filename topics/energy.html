⚡ Conservation of Energy and Momentum

🔋 Energy and Its Forms

Energy is the capacity to do work or cause change. It exists in many forms and can be transformed from one type to another, but the total amount of energy in an isolated system remains constant.

Forms of Energy

Kinetic

Energy of motion
KE = ½mv²

Potential

Stored energy
PE = mgh

Thermal

Heat energy
Random motion

Chemical

Stored in bonds
Food, fuel

Electrical

Moving charges
Current flow

🔄 Energy Transformations

Energy can change from one form to another, but it cannot be created or destroyed. This is the Law of Conservation of Energy.

🌟 CONSERVATION OF ENERGY 🌟
Einitial = Efinal
Energy can be transformed but never created or destroyed

Pendulum: Energy Transformation Example

Pendulum Energy Analysis:
  • At the highest points: All potential energy (PE = mgh), no kinetic energy
  • At the lowest point: All kinetic energy (KE = ½mv²), minimum potential energy
  • Total mechanical energy remains constant throughout the swing
  • In reality, some energy is lost to air resistance and friction (becomes thermal energy)

📉 Energy Efficiency

Efficiency measures how much useful energy is obtained from the total energy input. In real systems, some energy is always "lost" as waste heat due to friction and other factors.

Efficiency = (Useful Energy Output / Total Energy Input) × 100%
η = (Eout / Ein) × 100%

Energy Efficiency Examples

💡 Incandescent Bulb

5% efficient
95% becomes heat

💡 LED Bulb

80% efficient
20% becomes heat

🚗 Car Engine

25% efficient
75% lost as heat

⚡ Electric Motor

90% efficient
10% lost as heat

Why isn't anything 100% efficient? In real systems, friction, air resistance, electrical resistance, and other factors always convert some useful energy into thermal energy (heat). This heat usually cannot be recovered for useful work, making perfect efficiency impossible.

🏃 Momentum and Its Conservation

Momentum is the quantity of motion an object has. It depends on both the object's mass and velocity.

Momentum = Mass × Velocity
p = mv (measured in kg⋅m/s)
🎯 CONSERVATION OF MOMENTUM 🎯
pinitial = pfinal
In the absence of external forces, total momentum remains constant

Collision Examples: Momentum Conservation

Interactive Collision Demonstration

Collision Analysis:
  • Before: Ball 1 (3kg at 6 m/s) + Ball 2 (2kg at rest) = 18 kg⋅m/s total
  • After: Ball 1 (3kg at 2 m/s) + Ball 2 (2kg at 6 m/s) = 6 + 12 = 18 kg⋅m/s total
  • Result: Momentum is conserved! The faster, lighter ball transfers momentum to the slower, heavier ball
